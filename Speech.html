When I want something, I have to try as hard as I can to get it, or else it isn’t going to be exactly what I was looking for in the first place. Cutting corners, being clever, or trying to make it something easier than what it is supposed to be either decreases the quality to a point where it becomes not worth the admittedly decreased effort, or the attempts backfire and you have to clean up your mess, spending as much time or more fixing what your timesaving measures broke. A long time ago, I had to make a math project. We had to create an experiment, gather data, and analyze it. I, being the creative little dirtbag I was, set up the experiment for my project so all I had to do to collect data was to play videogames for hours on end. That is what I did, and by luck, I got an A on it! Now, fast forward. Another project appears on the horizon, and I think to myself, “Let’s do it again!” But I couldn’t do the same experiment twice. I had to think, and hard. I thought of an idea, real easy, really fun, but makes no sense. I work as hard as I can to make it make sense, so I can actually Balogna Sandwich my way through another project by sitting back and playing games. That takes me a few weeks, and I am starting to sweat. I realize I am wasting time, the deadline is approaching fast, and I rush out a completely different, although more mundane idea, and work through half of it before even showing the teacher my new conventional idea. I get about halfway through it, and learn I did it all wrong. I would have to start again, and this time with such little time left that I had no choice but to go back to the easy method, because I literally did not have the resources to do it correctly at all. And so, I was back to the old method, grasping at straws and having worked for weeks trying to make it easier with nothing having been done. I have to begin anew, confused about what to do, unsure if I will be able to complete it at all. Just in trying to cut corners and make it easier for myself, I have ended up with nothing but worthless effort. And so, I stand here before you all, having worked my behind off procrastinating for weeks, with my project still not started, and the entire assignment due tomorrow.
